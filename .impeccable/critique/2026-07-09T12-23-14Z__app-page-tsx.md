## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: Not shameless surface-level slop — DESIGN.md's flat, two-color, one-typeface, one-button-shape regime is followed with real discipline (no shadow, no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no eyebrow labels, no 01/02/03 numbering, no side-stripe cards). But underneath that discipline sits the exact generic SaaS-landing skeleton: hero+headline+subline+CTA, three icon-badges-in-a-row with a connector line, a 2×2 grid of identical white cards, then a form. Most concrete tell: the "AI matcht tegen je CV-pool" step icon is a four-point sparkle/star — the industry-standard "AI magic" glyph (Gemini/Copilot-style) — a direct violation of PRODUCT.md's own anti-reference against AI-startup-clichés.

**Deterministic scan**: `detect.mjs --json app components` — exit code 0, **zero findings**. Clean scan across all seven source files (`app/layout.tsx`, `app/page.tsx`, and all five components). No false positives to report since there were no findings to cross-check.

**Visual overlays**: Not available this run — browser screenshot and live-detector-injection both failed on a Chrome-extension/debugger conflict specific to this sandbox (see note above), not a page defect. DOM text extraction confirmed the page renders its real content (hero, three-step section, four benefit cards, four-field form, footer) with one interactive link (`Meld je aan` → `#aanmelden`).

## Overall Impression

A disciplined, on-brand execution of a template shape. The visual system (navy/teal, flat, one button, one card) is applied with real rigor and reads as considered rather than default — but the underlying structure is the same landing page as every other SaaS tool, and the page's single conversion mechanism (`mailto:`) has a silent, total failure mode that undermines the entire point of the site for a meaningful share of visitors. Fix the conversion path first; the visual polish is already ahead of the functional foundation.

## What's Working

- **System discipline**: button and card styling are literally the same classes reused per DESIGN.md spec (`Hero.tsx` CTA vs. `SignupForm.tsx` submit button; `Benefits.tsx` card matches border/rounded-lg/p-6 exactly).
- **Copy quality**: the hero headline "Te veel CV's. Te weinig tijd. Wij lossen dat op." is sharp, problem-first, jargon-free — genuinely matches PRODUCT.md's "directness over persuasion tricks" principle rather than generic SaaS marketing language.
- **Edge-case competence**: `lib/mailto.ts` only appends the "Toelichting" block when the field is non-empty, avoiding a blank section in the outgoing email — small but correctly handled.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] The entire conversion flow depends on unverified `mailto:`**
- Why it matters: `SignupForm.tsx` runs `window.location.href = href` unconditionally on submit and immediately shows "Bedankt voor je aanmelding!" with zero guarantee a mail client actually opened. On any device without a configured desktop mail client (browser-only Gmail/Outlook users, Chromebooks, locked-down corporate laptops — all common among the target recruiter audience) the site's single conversion goal fails completely and silently while the visitor sees a false success message. The "click here if your mail client didn't open" fallback link is itself another `mailto:` link, so it doesn't fix the same failure mode.
- Fix: replace or supplement with a submission path that doesn't depend on OS mail configuration — at minimum a copy-to-clipboard fallback with the email address, ideally a server-side POST to an API route / Supabase table / email-sending service.
- Suggested command: `/impeccable harden`

**[P1] The AI-match step icon is exactly the cliché PRODUCT.md bans**
- Why it matters: `HowItWorks.tsx`'s `MatchIcon` (four-point sparkle + small circle) is the industry-standard "AI magic" glyph used everywhere "AI" appears in copy — a direct violation of PRODUCT.md design principle 2/3 ("no AI-startup clichés").
- Fix: replace with an icon that more literally depicts comparison/matching (e.g. two overlapping documents, a balance/compare icon), in the same outline style as the other two icons.
- Suggested command: `/impeccable clarify` or `/impeccable colorize` (icon-level fix, small scope — could also just be done directly)

**[P1] The only CTA button fails WCAG AA contrast**
- Why it matters: teal (#0D9488) background with white text — used on literally every call-to-action on the page (`Hero.tsx` and `SignupForm.tsx` submit button share the same classes). Calculated contrast ≈ 3.74:1 against the required 4.5:1 for normal text (16px/semibold-600 does not qualify as "large text" under WCAG, which requires weight 700). PRODUCT.md explicitly commits to WCAG AA.
- Fix: darken teal (e.g. toward #0B7A70) and re-verify with a contrast checker.
- Suggested command: `/impeccable audit`

**[P2] No brand identity above the fold, no way back to the CTA**
- Why it matters: there's no logo/wordmark until the visitor reaches the Footer, eight sections later; `Hero.tsx` has no header, and DESIGN.md deliberately has no nav bar. A convinced visitor after "Hoe het werkt"/"Voordelen" can only get back to signup by manually scrolling.
- Fix: a minimal sticky bar (wordmark + the existing "Meld je aan" button) that appears once the hero is scrolled past — no full nav needed, just identity + the already-existing CTA shape.
- Suggested command: `/impeccable layout`

**[P2] Error messages don't clear reactively**
- Why it matters: `errors` state in `SignupForm.tsx` is only recalculated inside `validate()`, called exclusively from `handleSubmit`. After a failed submit, a red error message stays visible even after the field has been corrected, until the next submit click — erodes trust in the validation.
- Fix: validate per-field on `onChange`/`onBlur`, or at minimum clear the specific field's error as soon as its value changes.
- Suggested command: `/impeccable harden`

**[P3] Teal link text on navy sits just under AA**
- Why it matters: the footer email link and the post-submit fallback link use teal-on-navy, calculated contrast ≈ 4.37:1 against the required 4.5:1. Same root cause as the button fix above — one token adjustment fixes both.
- Suggested command: `/impeccable audit`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (Confused First-Timer)**: Sees no brand name above the fold — has to read on or scroll to the footer to confirm "this is TalentChart". Gets convinced after "Hoe het werkt", but the Hero CTA has since scrolled away with no sticky access — must manually scroll all the way down. After submitting, sees "Bedankt voor je aanmelding!" while, on a browser-only mail setup, nothing was actually sent — Jordan wrongly believes signup succeeded (ties to P0).

**Riley (Deliberate Stress Tester)**: Enters whitespace-only into "Naam" — correctly caught by `.trim().length === 0`. Enters an invalid email — regex catches it correctly. Corrects the name field after a failed attempt and expects the error to disappear immediately; it doesn't (P2) — trust in the validation drops. Double-clicks "Versturen" quickly — no disabled/loading state on the button, so no visual confirmation or protection against a duplicate click.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent User)**: Benefits from correct `htmlFor`/`id` pairing on all four fields — a genuine strength. But error messages aren't wired via `aria-describedby`/`aria-invalid` — a screen reader focusing the input after a failed submit won't automatically announce "Vul je naam in." Sam has to discover the error visually or via reading order. And the near-failing button contrast (P1, ~3.74:1) hits low-vision users hardest — exactly the kind of near-miss that's easy to overlook on pure visual review.

## Minor Observations

- No `autocomplete` attributes on name/company/email fields — easy browser-autofill win left on the table.
- No `aria-invalid`/`aria-describedby` linking fields to their error messages.
- The HowItWorks connector line (`teal/30`) is a clean, restrained application of the "Signal Teal Line" spec from DESIGN.md — correctly implemented.
- No visible brand name on the page itself (only in `<head>` metadata) — reinforces the P2 identity finding above.
- Footer year via `new Date().getFullYear()` — tidy, maintenance-free, no issue.
- No skip-link/back-to-top — not urgent for a page this length (~4-5 screens).

## Questions to Consider

1. Has this `mailto:` signup flow ever been tested on a locked-down corporate laptop or Chromebook without a default mail client? If not, what share of the actual recruiter audience can even complete this "form"?
2. If there are no customer logos/testimonials yet because there simply are no customers yet — should the copy itself acknowledge that honestly, rather than presenting a fully polished trust page that silently skips the proof step?
3. DESIGN.md explicitly bans a third accent color and any AI cliché — was the sparkle icon on the match step ever separately evaluated for "does this feel like AI slop," or did the discipline on color/shadow elsewhere create a false sense of safety that let this icon choice slip through?
